Reimagning High School Enrollment

PURPOSE OF REPORT

The Sacramento City Unified School Board asked us to investigate whether transforming HPHS into a grades 7-12 integrated middle school-high school – creating a “community learning center” K-12 pathway on one block, from a K-6 Leataata Floyd Elementary to a grades 7-12 Health Professions Middle/High School - would benefit Sacramento students and families, especially those in the Upper Land Park neighborhood around those schools.

Background

SACRAMENTO CITY UNIFIED SCHOOL DISTRICT

Sacramento City Unified School District (SCUSD) is one of the largest and most diverse public school districts in the Western United States.

Of the District’s 43,000 students, roughly 2 in 5 are Hispanic or Latino/a, while 1 in 5 are White, 1 in 5 are African American, and 1 in 5 are Asian. Sacramento City USD families speak more than 40 languages. 38% of District students do not speak English at home.

LEATAATA FLOYD ELEMENTARY SCHOOL

Immediately next door to Health Professions High is Leataata Floyd Elementary School.

A Superintendent’s Priority School and one of the most challenging elementary schools in the entire state of California, scoring in the bottom X% in student performance statewide, and the bottom %X in Sacramento City USD .

X% of Floyd Elementary students receive free or reduced lunch because of their families’ low income status . Leataata Floyd Elementary School is the neighborhood school for the Marina Vista and Alder Grove public housing communities, which total 751 residences with 1,900 residents , just across the street. The school serves, almost exclusively, children from these communities.

There is widespread deep concern from every corner of the district that SCUSD education is “failing [Floyd] families. ”

In and around the school, there is a common perception that the purpose of Floyd Elementary’s creation was and is to segregate these public housing children from the rest of the district.

Upper Land Park Neighborhood

The area around Health Professions High and Floyd Elementary is known primarily for the two large public housing communities that dominate the area. These two communities, Marina Vista (also known as Seavey Circle) and Alder Grove (also known as New Helvetia), are in the Upper Land Park or Broadway neighborhood of Sacramento. For the purposes of this report, these neighborhood names all refer to the areas around Floyd Elementary and Health Professions High.

The Mill at Broadway development, directly next door to both school, will include a public market, and 1,000 residences. Home are currently for sale from $200,000 to the “low $400,000s” per unit . Sales and marketing are targeted towards millennials. Units are on sale now, and The Mill’s developer has already contributed funding and work to build a community garden for Leataata Floyd Elementary.

THE IDEA – A COMMUNITY LEARNING CENTER

The grade 7-12 middle-high school model at Health Professions High is intriguing possible “two birds with one stone” solution – one that could provide an alternative, better “community learning center” pathway to graduation for Leataata Floyd Elementary’s students while also consistently supporting enrollment at Arthur A. Benjamin Health Professions High School. A community learning center on the same block as a K-12 pathway could centralize community services and support programs for students in one location.

We studied this idea for six months to weigh the benefits and risks of adding grades 7 and 8 to Health Professions High School, and make a recommendation to the Sacramento City Unified School Board about what to do.

We reviewed expert research on the topic from academic journals and newspapers nationwide.

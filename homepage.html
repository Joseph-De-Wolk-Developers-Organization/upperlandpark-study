Should Arthur A. Benjamin Health Professions High School become a grades 7 through 12 school?

{

PURPOSE OF REPORT

The Sacramento City Unified School Board in Sacramento, California asked us to study whether the school district should add grades 7 and 8 to Arthur A. Benjamin Health Profession High School in Upper Land Park, a historically underserved, low-income neighborhood.

Together with the K-6 Leataata Floyd Elementary School next door, this would create a streamlined K-12 pathway on one block for neighborhood families – a promising idea. It could also bolster the low enrollment rate at Health Profession High, a high performing school.

Our research brought up questions about segregation, trauma, and family choice we didn’t anticipate. Our answer surprised us – and it may surprise you.

Conclusion

The answer: no. For reasons even we didn’t foresee when we started this study, the risks of the proposal outweigh the likely benefits.

We found there are other simpler, cheaper, and more efficient actions the Sacramento City School Board can take to create many of the benefits of the K-12 community learning center for Upper Land Park.

We recommend the District should not add middle school grades to Health Professions High School without first taking steps to:

  • Help Health Professions High and neighboring Floyd Elementary work more closely together;
  • Make sure Upper Land Park families know Health Professions High is an option available to them that graduated 98% of its 2016 senior class, and sent 85% of those graduates to higher education.
  • Investigate the “segregation” and “trauma” reported by families and educators in Upper Land Park that undermines the fundamental learning and recruitment environment in and around those schools.

How We Reached Our Conclusion

A new K-12 pathway in Upper Land Park is unlikely to create an education pathway better than existing options, for three main reasons:

01.

There is a risk of increasing segregation of Upper Land Park families.

Adding a middle school on the same block with Floyd Elementary and Health Professions High would attract families from the surrounding untderserved communities (the same families that dominate Floyd Elementary), but is unlikely to be attractive to families from outside this neighborhood. The result could be isolating Upper Land Park from other Sacramento City families in a school pathway that keeps students just blocks from their homes in this public housing for the majority of the first 18 years of their lives.

 
 

“You move those services… people that need those services will follow those services. Same with schools. ‘Sorry we can't meet your needs here. But let me give you [the Principal of Health Professions High’s phone] number, she’s really great’…. We make it so easy…. It’s gonna create segregation."

- C.K. McClatchy High School educator

 
02.

It’s risky to force Health Professions High to manage several major school changes all at once.

Adding grades 7 and 8 to Health Professions High would force the school to deal with several big changes to school culture all at the same time, increasing the risk of implementing the new model well. The changes include:

  1. Adding 12 and 13 year olds to a high school campus;
  2. Adding programs and supports for increasing numbers of students from surrounding Marina Vista and Alder Grove public housing communities;
  3. Managing increasing teacher workload and likely increased teacher turnover;
  4. Shifting from recruiting 9th graders to recruiting 7th and 9th graders;
  5. Possible doubling in overall students.
 
 

"If that school wants to be successful they have to cater to those kids in that area.”

- Upper Land Park community member

 
03.

It probably won’t increase Health Professions High enrollment.

Adding middle school grades does not address the top parent concerns causing Health Professions High’s low enrollment in the first place:

  • Missing out on the “traditional high school experience” – Health Professions High is a small school with small sports programs, dances, etc.
  • The safety of the Upper Land Park area;
  • The narrowness of the health professions pathway.

Our Recommendations

Adding grades 7 and 8 to Health Professions High does not solve Floyd Elementary students’ underperformance, or Health Professions High’s low enrollment.

Yet, at the same time, the status quo in Upper Land Park is not acceptable.

We are urging the Sacramento City School Board to take action to create better education options for Upper Land Park families.
